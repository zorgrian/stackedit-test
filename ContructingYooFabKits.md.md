# Constructing a YooFab-Style Kit

**Author:** Geoffrey David Cowne – MØOOZ

At YooFab, we champion a philosophy of clean, robust, and accessible electronics construction. While our method shares a conceptual link with the G-QRP club's “Limerick” approach — using the printed circuit board (PCB) as a primary structural element — our technique has distinct advantages that define the YooFab style.

The core principle is simple: the PCB is **not** folded into a box, but is instead precision-mounted onto a durable metal enclosure. This combines the electrical functionality of the board with the superior mechanical strength and shielding of a metal case.

## The YooFab Difference — A Clean Exterior

A key differentiation of our method is the placement of the circuit tracks.

**G-QRP Limerick Style:** Often uses tracks on both sides of the PCB, including the exterior-facing side that becomes the outside of the folded box.

**YooFab Style:** We design our boards so that **all** copper tracks and the majority of components are located on the *inside* of the assembled unit. When the PCB is mounted onto its metal base, the outward-facing surface presents a clean, professional finish of a black surface with a white legend. This is where all controls go. (On/ Off switch — Rotary controls — etc.) This looks superior and protects the circuit from accidental damage and fingerprints.

## What You Will Need

**Before you begin construction, ensure you have the correct tools:**

*   **Soldering Iron:** A temperature-controlled soldering iron with a heating element of approximately 50 Watts is highly recommended. This provides the ideal thermal mass for effective soldering.
*   **Solder:** We advise using good quality **60/40 multicore solder**. This type of solder flows easily and creates reliable joints.
*   **Basic Tools:** Side cutters, tweezers, a multimeter, and a small screwdriver set.

## Step-by-Step Construction Guide

Follow these steps to build your YooFab kit successfully.

### Step 1: Prepare the Components

Using the parts list that is provided, lay out every component from the kit and identify it. Checking off each component as you find it is a good idea. Become familiar with the PCB, taking note of the silkscreen labels that show the locations of each component.

### Step 2: Prepare the Enclosure

A metal box that will serve as the chassis is included in your kit. Before mounting the PCB, make sure that any switches, sockets, or connectors on the front panel are inserted into the appropriate holes.

### Step 3: solder the Components

The most important stage is this one. Work methodically, usually beginning with the flattest and smallest parts (diodes, resistors) and working your way up to the taller ones (capacitors, connectors).

**The Best Soldering Practices:**

*   **Insert the Component:** Place the component legs through the correct holes on the PCB.
*   **Bend the Legs:** Slightly bend the legs on the solder-side to hold the component in place.
*   **Apply Heat and Solder:** Touch the soldering iron tip to both the component leg and the PCB pad simultaneously. After a second or two, apply the solder to the joint — not directly to the iron. The joint should be shiny and concave, establishing a “volcano” shape.
*   **Trim the Legs:** Once the joint has cooled, use your side cutters to trim the excess component leg.

### Step 4: Mount the PCB to the Enclosure

Once all components are soldered, and you have done a visual inspection, it is time to mount the board. The PCB will attach to the metal box using stand-offs and screws.

*   Position the PCB accurately over the mounting points on the enclosure.
*   Secure it firmly using the provided screws. This connection also serves to ground the PCB to the metal case, improving the kit's electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) performance.

### Step 5: Final Assembly and Testing

Complete any final internal connections, such as wiring from the PCB to front-panel controls. Attach the lid of the enclosure. Consult the kit's specific documentation for instructions on initial power-on and testing procedures. Always test the kit cautiously the first time it is powered.

## Why the YooFab style Works

*   **Professional Finish:** The clean exterior of the PCB provides a look that rivals commercially manufactured equipment.
*   **Superior Shielding:** The metal enclosure offers excellent shielding against external electrical noise, which is crucial for sensitive radio equipment like our Amplified Loop Antenna and future DSB transceiver.
*   **Mechanical Robustness:** The rigid metal base protects the PCB from flexing and damage, ensuring long-term reliability.
*   **Simplified Construction:** By avoiding complex folding, the build process is more straightforward and less prone to error, making it ideal for constructors of all experience levels.

**Enjoy building your YooFab kit — where thoughtful design meets practical construction.**
